## Hermann Goering

  

#### Notable actions:  
  

- Goering joined the Nazi party after hearing Hitler in a speech in 1922.
    
- Soon after in 1923, he was given the title Oberster-SA Fuhrer; the leader of the SA.
    
- Headed the Four-Year Plan (1936), focusing on preparing Germany's economy for war.
    
- Played a key role in the Reichstag Fire Decree in 1933, which helped Hitler consolidate power.
    
- Was head of the Luftwaffe.
    
- Ordered the use of the Gestapo to eliminate enemies of the state.
    

#### Nuremberg Trials:

- At the Nuremberg Trials, he was sentenced to death, but committed suicide before being killed.
    

## Joseph Goebbels

  

#### Notable actions:

- Was appointed the role of Minister of Propaganda in Nazi Germany by Hitler, shortly after the great depression of 1930.
    
- He controlled all forms of media in Nazi Germany.
    
- Promoted anti-semetism, and organised the Kristallnacht pogrom.
    
- Founded the Nazi propaganda newspaper “Das Reich”.
    

#### Nuremberg Trials:

- Committed suicide in Hitler’s bunker, along with his wife and Children in 1945.
    

## Heinrich Himmler

#### Notable actions:

- Joined the Nazi Party in 1923, took part in the Beer Hall Putsch.
    
- Was appointed the role of the Head of the SS (Schutzstaffel) in 1925.
    
- Also was the head of the Gestapo (secret police).
    
- He was also the primary architect of the Holocaust, and concentration camps.
    

#### Nuremberg Trials (Not applicable):

- Captured by the British forces in 1945, and committed suicide.
    

## Ernst Rohm

#### Notable actions:

- Was appointed leader of the SA in 1930 after Hitler requested him to do so.
    
- Advocated for the SA to have more power.
    
- Was famously a homosexual, who Hitler ‘tolerated’.
    

#### Nuremberg Trials (Not applicable):

- Was executed on the Night of the Long Knives in 1934, as he was seen as a threat.
    

## Rudolf Hess

#### Notable actions:

- Was appointed Deputy Fuhrer in 1933.
    
- Was a wealthy university graduate.
    
- Financed the SA in the early days.
    
- Also helped Hitler write Mein Kampf.
    
- Tried to negotiate peace with Britain 1941.
    

#### Nuremberg Trials (Not applicable):

- Died in Spandau prison in 1987, allegedly hanged himself in his library.
    
- Some consider it might have been a murder, considering he was 94 years old when doing this.
    

## Reinhard Heydrich


#### Notable actions:

- Was the main architect of ‘Final solution’.
    
- He was in charge of the Kripo (Berlin Police Force), and the SD, along with a senior position in the Gestapo.
    
- Led the Reich Security Main Office (RSHA) and helped coordinate the Holocaust.
    

#### Nuremberg Trials (Not applicable):

- Assassinated by Czech resistance fighters in 1942; his death led to severe reprisals by the Nazis.
    


## Hjalmar Schacht

  

#### Notable actions:

- Minister of Economics from 1934-1937.
    
- Helped stabilise the German economy after the Great Depression.
    
- Disagreed with Hitler’s military spending, so was removed in 1937.
    

#### Nuremberg Trials:

- Plead not guilty for ‘Conspiracy’ of planning wars of aggression.
    
- Was sentenced to 8 years in prison.
    

## Albert Speer

  

#### Notable actions:

- Hitler’s chief architect, who designed many of the monumental buildings planned for the Nazi regime.
    
- Improved efficiency in arms production during World War II, significantly aiding the German war effort.
    
- Claimed to have been unaware of the Holocaust, although this is widely debated.
    

#### Nuremberg Trials:

- Pleaded innocent, said he had no idea of Concentration Camps.
    
- Sentenced to 20 years in prison.
